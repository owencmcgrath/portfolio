in the history of great automotive blunders, it is hard to put stellantis' late 2010s through mid 2020s run anywhere other than the top three. it's a classic case of marketers and executives listening to shareholders instead of their customers, ultimately resulting in a declining share price, dying brands, and waning consumer sentiment.

it's important to note how stellantis came to be: like all great, successful, and remembered companies, m&a! during the financial crisis, chrysler filed for bankruptcy and was eventually bailed out by the auto-unions, the u.s. government, and most importantly (for our story) the fiat group at 58.5% of the company (merger completed in 2014).

they had a solid run for a few years, with cars like the jeep wrangler propelling the company into a renaissance period, or as much of a renaissance as hodge-podge of poorly defined brands can really have. however, after falsifying emissiosn tests (because that worked so well for vw) they merged with PSA to form Stellantis.

so, what is stellantis supposed to do? they bled 3 billion last year and a lot of these brands have lineups that are long in the tooth. since i am an american, i am only really going to focus on the brands that we see stateside (alfa, chrysler, dodge/ram, fiat, jeep, maserati)

alfa/maserati

both of these brands are incredibly historied with alfa romeo's four le man wins and maserati's gt1 competition with the mc12. however, both of these oems have aged product line-ups that are no longer appealing to the modern buyer. most people do not care about a sporty sedan, even if the guilia quad is both gorgeous and recieved fantastic reviews. neither suv line-up is compelling either, but the platform shared alfa tonale is an interesting entry into the italian manufacturer. ultimately, i think these two brands need to merge underneath the alfa romeo brand-name. maserati has some connotations about reliability, and while alfa romeo isn't much better, it's a start. retain but refresh the guilia, replace the stelvio with the grecale, kill every maserati sedan and sports car, and keep the tonale as an entry point into the brand.

chrysler

nothing screams "luxury brand" quite like minivans. this one's easy: kill the brand entirely and bring back the dodge journey.

dodge/ram

dodge as a manufacturer made one of the most beloved muscle cars of the modern era (the charger/challenger duo) and then promptly killed it for an electric, awd gt car. it's this level of decision making that really makes you wonder if anyone at stellantis has any clue what their customers want. the electric charger (potentially the worst possible seo of all time) was so poorly reviewed that they started out by telling journalists to ignore the ev because an ice version was coming. despite this, dodge has some quality products in the hornet (the underpinnings of the alfa tonale) and the durango. however, the durango is due for a refresh
